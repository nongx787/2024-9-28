Hagia Sophia, the main church of the Byzantine Empire, located in Istanbul, Turkey,
has a history of nearly 1,500 years, famous for its huge dome, is a representative
of Byzantine architecture, the central church of Orthodox Christianity, and a
monument to the heyday of the Byzantine Empire.
Hagia Sophia is centralized, with a length of 77.0 meters from east to west, 71.0
meters from north to south, and a height of 54.8 meters. It was the third church
built by order of the Byzantine Emperor Justinian I in 532 AD. Designed by the
physicist Isidore of Miletus and the mathematician Antimus of Trallus, it was
completed in 537 AD with the support of the Byzantine state. After 1453, it was
occupied by the Turks and converted into a mosque. It was converted into a museum
in 1935 and listed as a World Heritage Site in 1985.
